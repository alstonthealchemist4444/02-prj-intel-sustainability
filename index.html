Building a Sustainable Future

Explore Intel’s sustainability journey with milestone stories that show how innovation and responsibility create a cleaner, greener tomorrow.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename the newly formed company NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of technological innovation.

This founding moment launched a culture of innovation that later supported Intel’s sustainable manufacturing and energy programs.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution and propelling the future of computing devices.

The 4004 set the stage for smaller, more efficient systems that would later enable data centers to become more energy efficient.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture that drives countless PCs and servers in the modern era.

The widespread adoption of x86 helped create economies of scale that later supported Intel’s investments in cleaner manufacturing.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture, ushering in a new era of performance and multitasking for personal computers.

Greater performance helped customers do more with less energy, an important step for sustainable computing over time.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

This year marks Intel's highest annual greenhouse gas emissions for operations. Over subsequent years, Intel invests heavily in chemical abatement, renewable energy, and energy-efficient manufacturing.

Intel’s response included tighter controls and new clean-tech systems that reduced emissions across the global supply chain.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling) strategy and 2030 goals, aiming to drive industry-wide progress on climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

The strategy created measurable goals that align Intel’s innovation with global sustainability priorities.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel announces its commitment to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 and 2) across its global operations by 2040.

This long-term commitment accelerated Intel’s shift toward renewable energy and lower-carbon manufacturing processes.

2023

Renewable Electricity

The company achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide, helping to drastically lower carbon emissions and drive progress toward Intel's long-term sustainability goals.

Renewable electricity helped Intel reduce operational impact while continuing to scale advanced manufacturing.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, uniting suppliers, government officials, and industry leaders to collaborate on next-generation sustainable semiconductor manufacturing.

The summit fostered cross-industry partnerships focused on reducing waste and improving energy efficiency worldwide.
